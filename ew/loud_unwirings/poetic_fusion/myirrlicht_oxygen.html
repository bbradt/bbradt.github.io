MYIRRLICH oxygen

oxygen

By: MyIrrlicht

Breathe cautiously at first;
the air is as choked with pollutants,
as it is wrapped in life-giving diamonds.

The trees hum alongside us,
the song of sweet gases passing over membranes,
their trade to us, just for living and balance,
and we make more breath,
with which we will fill our rooms
until there’s nothing left ;
a spark at any moment might set it all ablaze


Breathe cautiously at first;
in time you’ll learn to do it brazenly,
and will forget the taste,
but for now, treasure it
as if it’s something given only to you.


The water’s full of it
and other things we threw away,
rising around our ankles,
searching for the place we used to hold ourselves
when we dipped ourselves into that ancient river;
we can’t breathe water-
it’s the price of usury.


Breathe cautiously at first(
there are only so many places you can do so,
and the void and the waters
encroach upon them constantly,
as do the mouths of others.
