MYIRRLICH Beryllium

Beryllium

By: MyIrrlicht

A placid surface,
undisturbed,
which did not posses itself,
having never skirted electric conversion,
shimmered,
and in the nothing blinked,
and suddenly,
nothing was never nothing again
and everything
was.

Everything blinked,
and in a moment,
saw itself,
and heard itself,
and felt itself
in an uncountable number of ways,
spinning and moving within itself
describing itself
creating itself
and the myths of itself
at first by accident and mechanism
and then,
by allowing itself
to seem to supersede itself,
until at last,
it reached the point of breath,
and dived into and among itself,
alive in and divided into and of itself completely,
until it intended itself
as everything.
